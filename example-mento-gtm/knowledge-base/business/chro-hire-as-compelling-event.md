# CHRO hire as compelling event

> When a Series B–D company hires its first CHRO, comp-band infrastructure becomes a board-level priority within 45–120 days. This is Mento's most reliable trigger.

## Why this works (claim)

A new CHRO at a Series B/C/D company inherits 3 things that drive Mento adoption:
1. **A compensation infrastructure they didn't build** — spreadsheets, no banding, no philosophy doc
2. **A board mandate to professionalize the people function** — visible deliverables expected by 90 days
3. **Authority to pick tools** — unlike VP People hires who often inherit decisions

## Evidence

- [VERIFIED: deal-history, 2026-Q1-Q4] Of the 23 closed-won deals in the last 4 quarters, 14 had a CHRO hire as the documented compelling event. Median time from CHRO start date → first Mento contact: 67 days.
- [VERIFIED: accounts/example-acme/timeline.md] Acme's CHRO Sarah K. was hired 2026-03-15. First outbound 2026-05-08. Reply within 18 hours.
- [INFERRED: pattern across 4 deals] CHRO hires at Series A (pre-scale) don't predict — they're often part-time or fractional and don't have budget authority yet.

## Edges

| If… | …then |
|---|---|
| Series B–D + CHRO hire in last 120 days | Tier 1, high-priority outbound |
| Series A + CHRO hire | Defer; usually fractional, no budget |
| Series E+ + CHRO hire | Likely a replacement, not a first; less reliable |
| Series B–D + VP People hire (not CHRO) | Tier 2 — still useful, less authority |

## When this stops being true

- If Mento's positioning shifts upmarket to enterprise, CHRO hires become less load-bearing (those orgs already have full comp infrastructure)
- If we see >3 consecutive CHRO-hire deals lose to spreadsheets, demote
