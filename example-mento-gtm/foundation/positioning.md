# Mento Positioning

> How Mento talks about itself, where it sits in the market, and what it sounds like when reps explain it cold.

## The one-liner

**Mento is the compensation platform for B2B SaaS people-leaders who have to defend every comp decision to their board.**

## The promise (what reps actually say in the first 30 seconds)

> *"You just hired a CHRO. Your next comp review is going to be the first one she has to defend to your board. Mento is what she stands up to defend it — bands sourced from real comparable companies, philosophy doc the founders can sign off on, and a workflow her HRBPs use weekly. We're priced for Series-B-to-D companies, not for Pave-tier enterprises."*

## Competitive map

| Competitor | Where they win | Where Mento wins |
|---|---|---|
| **Pave** | Enterprise, post-Series-D, large in-house teams | Mid-market (B–D), where Pave is over-spec'd and over-priced |
| **Compa** | Specifically commercial roles, sales comp | Cross-functional comp, not commercial-only |
| **Carta Total Comp** | Companies already deep in Carta | Companies that want comp as a *first-class* tool, not a Carta add-on |
| **Spreadsheets + Radford** | Anywhere there's no people-leader yet | The moment a CHRO/VP-People lands |
| **DIY in BambooHR / Rippling** | < 150 headcount, founder still eyeballing it | 150+ where board defensibility starts mattering |

## The three buyer personas

| Persona | What they care about | What kills the deal |
|---|---|---|
| **CHRO (champion)** | Defensibility, time-to-stand-up, vendor reliability | Anything that looks like "more spreadsheets" |
| **CFO (signer)** | TCO, comparable-company data quality, audit trail | "We tried Pave and it was too expensive" baseline lingering |
| **Founder/CEO (final blessing)** | Speed-to-implementation, board narrative | A demo that's longer than 25 minutes |

## What Mento sounds like in writing

- **Direct.** No "transform your compensation strategy." Just *"the comp platform CHROs use to defend their board reviews."*
- **Numeric where it can be.** Comparable-company benchmarks are the moat. Show numbers.
- **Founder-readable.** The CFO and CEO read these docs too. No HR jargon.

## What Mento does *not* claim

- Not a payroll provider
- Not an HRIS
- Not a performance management tool
- Not a recruiting comp tool (focused on retention/refresh cycles, not offer-stage)
- Not enterprise-priced — explicitly priced for B–D companies under 800 headcount

## Trigger-to-positioning map

When a specific trigger fires, the pitch tightens to the trigger:

| Trigger | The line |
|---|---|
| **CHRO hire** | "Your next comp review is the first one she has to defend. We're the platform she stands it up on." |
| **Series C/D close** | "Your last comp review was pre-this-round. Your new investors are going to expect a defensible refresh." |
| **Headcount delta ≥10%** | "You added 30 people in 90 days. The bands you set last year are now wrong on the bottom and the top." |
| **Job-board surge for senior IC roles** | "You're hiring senior. The offer-to-band drift problem starts here — we close it." |
