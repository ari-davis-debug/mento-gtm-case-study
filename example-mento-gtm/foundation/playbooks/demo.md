# Demo Playbook

> 25 minutes. Never longer. Mento founders demo by saying less, not more.

## The structure

| Time | What happens | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–2:00 | Re-state what we heard in discovery | They want to know you listened |
| 2:00–5:00 | Show the comp-band view sourced from real comparable companies | The single most differentiating screen |
| 5:00–12:00 | Walk one comp scenario in their company's shape (use their headcount + their funding stage) | Concrete > abstract |
| 12:00–18:00 | Show the philosophy doc generation — board-ready output | This is what CHROs stress about |
| 18:00–22:00 | Pricing + implementation timeline | Numbers + dates, no fluff |
| 22:00–25:00 | Next-step ask | Always have one |

## What to never demo

- The "everything we do" overview
- Generic comparable-company data (always use a company *they* would compare to)
- HRIS-style features that aren't comp-specific
- A demo > 25 minutes — they'll disengage, and you'll lose the close

## The 3 buyer-specific takes

| Buyer | What you emphasize | Where you skip |
|---|---|---|
| **CHRO** | Defensibility, time-to-band-refresh, HRBP workflow | Skip pricing in detail — they punt to CFO |
| **CFO** | TCO, audit trail, vendor reliability, comparable-company data integrity | Skip the philosophy doc — they don't care |
| **Founder/CEO** | Speed-to-stand-up, board narrative, "how does this land in the next board meeting" | Skip workflow detail — they want the outcome |

## After the demo

Recap email within 1 hour. Three bullets:
1. What they specifically asked about (verbatim)
2. The next concrete action (with a date)
3. One thing you'll send them by tomorrow (a benchmark, a one-pager, a customer ref)

Log a 3-line summary to `accounts/<slug>/notes.md`. If their reaction surfaced a new pattern, propose a synthesis update.

## When to walk away

If during the demo you hear any of these, slow the close and re-qualify:
- *"We're going to need to run this by procurement."* (Likely 4+ month cycle — not Mento's shape)
- *"Can we get a 12-month pilot at 50% off?"* (Buying-pressure mismatch — usually means no budget)
- *"What if we just used spreadsheets for now?"* (Trigger isn't salient yet — pull back, re-trigger in 60 days)
