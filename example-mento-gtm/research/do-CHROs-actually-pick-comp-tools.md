# Do CHROs actually pick comp tools?

## Question

When we say "CHRO hires drive Mento purchases," is the CHRO actually the *buyer*, or are they the *champion* and the CFO/COO is the buyer? This matters because if it's the latter, our outbound is mis-targeted.

## Why it matters

If CHROs are champions but not buyers, we should:
- Add a CFO/COO secondary touch
- Reframe the playbook from "win the CHRO" to "give the CHRO the CFO-ready ROI doc"
- Adjust the eval gate to score "would this work for a CFO to forward?"

## Runs

### Run r-2026-04-15-1100
**Source**: Avoma transcripts from 14 closed-won deals in 2026 Q1
**Method**: Searched for who said "let's move forward" or who signed
**Finding**: 10 of 14 — the CHRO was the explicit decision-maker. 4 of 14 — the CFO had veto power that they didn't exercise. 0 of 14 — the CFO actively chose Mento.
**Confidence**: Medium — small sample, possible selection bias (these are our wins)

### Run r-2026-04-18-1500
**Source**: Lost-deal post-mortems Q1 2026
**Method**: Searched lost-reason field for "CFO pushed back" or similar
**Finding**: 3 of 6 lost deals had explicit CFO veto. Of those, 2 cited budget; 1 cited "we use Pave."
**Confidence**: Medium

### Run r-2026-04-22-0930
**Source**: Sarah K. (Acme CHRO) reply email + behavior
**Method**: Observed what she said yes/no to in the first 18 hours
**Finding**: She booked the demo without consulting the CFO. The reply email said "I have authority to bring in tools under $100k ARR." This validates the win-pattern.
**Confidence**: High for this one account; one data point only

## Synthesis (current, as of 2026-04-22)

CHROs ARE the buyer at our deal size (sub-$100k ARR) and at our buyer profile (Series B–D). The CFO becomes the buyer above ~$150k ARR or in companies where the CFO has explicitly centralized tool purchases.

**Implication**: Outbound stays targeted at CHRO. Add a follow-up sequence for CHRO → CFO handoff *only* if deal size projects above $100k or if the CHRO has been at the company <30 days (still building credibility internally).

## Open questions

- Does this hold above $100k ARR? We don't have enough data.
- What about companies where the CHRO reports to a COO, not the CEO? (Reporting line might predict authority.)
- Does it change if the CFO is ex-comp-consulting? (Sarah K. mentioned her last CFO was — they had strong opinions.)
